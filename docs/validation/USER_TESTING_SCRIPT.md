# Coach V6 User Testing Script

## Test Information

**Test Duration:** 20-25 minutes per session  
**Participants:** 10 SMB owners (mix of industries)  
**Method:** Remote (Zoom) with screen sharing  
**Incentive:** $100 Amazon gift card  

---

## Pre-Session Setup (5 minutes before)

**Moderator Checklist:**

- [ ] Zoom link sent and confirmed
- [ ] Figma prototype link ready
- [ ] Recording permission obtained
- [ ] Incentive code ready to send
- [ ] Note-taking document open
- [ ] Timer ready

**Environment:**

- Quiet room, good internet
- Two monitors: One for Zoom, one for notes
- Backup: Phone ready if Zoom fails

---

## Introduction (2 minutes)

**Script:**

> "Hi [Name], thank you so much for joining today! I'm [Your Name] from Dyocense.
>
> We're designing a new interface for business owners like you, and I'd love to get your honest feedback. There are absolutely no wrong answers - in fact, critical feedback is the most valuable.
>
> I'll show you a prototype and ask you to complete a few tasks while thinking aloud. Tell me what you're thinking, what confuses you, what you like or don't like.
>
> Before we start, I need to record this session so my team can review it later. Is that okay with you? Great, starting recording now.
>
> Do you have any questions before we begin?"

---

## Warm-Up Questions (3 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand their business context and current tools

**Q1:** "Tell me a bit about your business. What do you do?"

- [ ] Note: Industry type
- [ ] Note: Revenue size (if they share)
- [ ] Note: Team size

**Q2:** "What tools do you currently use to manage your business finances or operations?"

- [ ] Note: Tools mentioned (QuickBooks, Excel, etc.)
- [ ] Note: Satisfaction level

**Q3:** "When you check in on your business health, what are the first things you look at?"

- [ ] Note: Metrics they care about
- [ ] Note: Frequency of checking
- [ ] Note: Time pressure context

---

## Part 1: First Impression Test (3 minutes)

**Setup:** Share screen with Figma prototype on home screen

**Script:**
> "I'm going to show you a new interface. Take 30 seconds to look around without clicking anything. Just absorb what you see."

**[Show Fitness Dashboard Home Screen - 30 seconds of silence]**

**Q1:** "What is this app trying to help you do?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Do they mention "health" or "fitness"?
- [ ] Do they mention "dashboard" or "overview"?
- [ ] Do they understand it's about their business?
- [ ] Confidence level (certain vs. guessing)

**Q2:** "What stands out most to you?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Health score mentioned?
- [ ] Red alerts noticed?
- [ ] Specific numbers or metrics?
- [ ] Visual elements (colors, layout)

**Q3:** "On a scale of 1-10, how clear is this interface? What's confusing?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Score: ___/10
- [ ] Specific confusion points
- [ ] Terminology issues

---

## Part 2: Priority Identification Task (2 minutes)

**Script:**
> "Imagine it's Tuesday morning, you're grabbing coffee before opening your store. You have 30 seconds to check this app. What's the most important thing you should focus on today?"

**[Start timer, observe without helping]**

**Metrics:**

- [ ] Time to answer: ___ seconds (target: <15s)
- [ ] Correct priority identified? (Cash flow alert)
- [ ] Confidence in answer (hesitant vs. certain)
- [ ] Path taken (eyes, cursor movement)

**Follow-up:** "How did you know that was the priority?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Visual cues mentioned (red color, position)
- [ ] Number-based reasoning (dollar amounts)
- [ ] Past experience patterns

---

## Part 3: Action Taking Task (3 minutes)

**Script:**
> "Let's say you want to address this cash flow issue. Show me what you would do."

**[Let them click through prototype]**

**Observe:**

- [ ] Do they find "Create Action Plan" button?
- [ ] Do they read the recommendations?
- [ ] Do they understand the suggested steps?
- [ ] Do they try to add to tasks?

**Follow-up Questions:**

**Q1:** "Would you actually do these steps in real life? Why or why not?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Practicality assessment
- [ ] Trust in AI recommendations
- [ ] Need for more information

**Q2:** "What would you need to know before taking action?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Data verification needs
- [ ] Authority/permission concerns
- [ ] Risk assessment

---

## Part 4: Health Score Deep Dive (3 minutes)

**Script:**
> "I noticed this 67/100 score at the top. What does that number mean to you?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Interpretation accuracy
- [ ] Emotional reaction (concern, curiosity, dismissal)
- [ ] Need for more context

**Q:** "The score dropped 8 points from last week. How does that make you feel?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Urgency perception
- [ ] Desire to understand why
- [ ] Action orientation

**Q:** "Click on the health score to see more details."

**[Let them explore the drill-down]**

**Observe:**

- [ ] Do they understand the components?
- [ ] Do they find the insights valuable?
- [ ] What questions do they ask?

---

## Part 5: Comparison Test (5 minutes)

**Setup:** Share screen with CURRENT chat interface (CoachV5)

**Script:**
> "Now let me show you the current version of this app for comparison."

**[Show current chat interface with conversation history]**

**Q1:** "Take a moment to explore this. What are the main differences you notice?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Chat vs. dashboard observation
- [ ] Information density comparison
- [ ] Navigation differences

**Q2:** "Which interface do you prefer, and why?"

**Critical Question - Note detailed response:**

- [ ] Preference: Fitness Dashboard / Current Chat / No Strong Preference
- [ ] Primary reason:
- [ ] Secondary reasons:

**Q3:** "In what situations would you prefer one over the other?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Use case differentiation
- [ ] Time pressure considerations
- [ ] Task-based preferences

**Q4:** "If you could combine the best of both, what would that look like?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Hybrid ideas
- [ ] Missing features
- [ ] Dealbreakers

---

## Part 6: Pricing Discussion (3 minutes)

**Script:**
> "Let's talk about value. The current chat version costs $75 per month."

**Q1:** "How much value does that provide to your business?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] ROI justification
- [ ] Price sensitivity
- [ ] Comparison to other tools

**Q2:** "The fitness dashboard version has more proactive insights, real-time alerts, and personalized recommendations. What would you be willing to pay for that?"

**Open-ended first, then prompt:**

- [ ] Initial response: $___
- [ ] If hesitant: "Would you pay $150/month?"
- [ ] If yes to $150: "What about $200/month?"
- [ ] If no to $150: "What about $100/month?"

**Final willing-to-pay:** $___/month

**Q3:** "What would make the premium pricing a 'no-brainer' for you?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Feature requests
- [ ] ROI requirements (save X hours, prevent Y losses)
- [ ] Comparison to value of alternatives

---

## Part 7: Open Feedback (3 minutes)

**Q1:** "What's the one thing you'd change about the fitness dashboard?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Usability issues
- [ ] Missing information
- [ ] Design preferences

**Q2:** "What would make you use this app every single day?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Habit triggers
- [ ] Push notification preferences
- [ ] Integration needs

**Q3:** "Is there anything I didn't ask about that you think I should know?"

**Look for:**

- [ ] Unexpected insights
- [ ] Competitive context
- [ ] Business-specific needs

---

**Script:**
> "This has been incredibly helpful, thank you! I'll send you the $100 Amazon gift card via email within 24 hours.
>
